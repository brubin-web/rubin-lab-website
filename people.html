Our Team

Meet the researchers driving innovation in microbiome editing.

Principal Investigator

Ben Rubin, Ph.D.

Principal Investigator

Leads the Rubin Lab at UC Berkeley's Innovative Genomics Institute. Developed technologies for CRISPR-Cas editing of microbiomes during postdoctoral work in Jennifer Doudna's lab. Now leads the BIOME initiative.

Lab Members

Postdoctoral Researchers

Lindsey Pieper

Postdoctoral Researcher

Earned her PhD in Biomedical Sciences from UCSF in 2023 where she investigated environmental factors impacting gut microbiome driven degradation of azo compounds (e.g. food colorings and IBD drug Sulfasalazine). In the Rubin lab, she works with the Audacious team to advance microbiome editing, focusing on how to select for edits within a community.

Bingliang Xie

Postdoctoral Researcher

Received a Ph.D. in Microbiology from the Institute of Microbiology, Chinese Academy of Sciences in 2023, where he developed gene-targeted methods to cultivate obligate epibiotic bacteria (Candidate Phyla Radiation bacteria).

Kuang Hu

Postdoctoral Researcher

A postdoctoral researcher in the BIOME initiative at UC Berkeley's Innovative Genomics Institute, working under Ben on discovery of novel RNA-guided editing tools.

Madeline Hayes

Postdoctoral Researcher (Joint with Cress Lab & O'Malley Lab)

Completed undergraduate studies at Colorado State University, then joined the Microbiology Doctoral Training Program at UW-Madison. Currently working with the Joint Bioenergy Institute to improve microbial lignin degradation for sustainable biofuel and chemical production.

Graduate Students

Sophia Swartz

Graduate Student

Working on investigating the regulation and integration mechanism of CRISPR-associated transposons to develop and optimize editing tools for non-model microbes. Co-advised by Jennifer Doudna and Brady Cress.

Leo Song

Graduate Student

Former lab manager who continued with the Rubin Lab as a graduate student through the UC Berkeley Comparative Biochemistry department.

Staff Scientists

Jigyasa Arora

Staff Scientist

Received her Ph.D. in Biology from the Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology (OIST). She bioinformatically examined host gut microbiome interactions in insects. In the Rubin Lab, she optimizes bioinformatics tools to investigate CRISPR-associated transposons regulation.
