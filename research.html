Our Research

Developing and applying cutting-edge tools to edit microbial communities for beneficial outcomes in environmental sustainability, agriculture, and human health.

Biodegradation & Production Microbiomes

We engineer microbial communities for sustainable biodegradation and bioproduction applications. By understanding and manipulating the complex interactions within microbial ecosystems, we develop solutions for breaking down environmental pollutants and producing valuable compounds.

Our research focuses on identifying key microbial players in degradation pathways, optimizing community compositions for enhanced activity, and developing scalable approaches for real-world applications in waste treatment and biomanufacturing.

Soil Microbial Editing

Soil microbiomes play a critical role in plant health, nutrient cycling, and carbon sequestration. We develop precision tools to modify soil microbial communities for improved agricultural outcomes while maintaining ecosystem stability.

Our approaches combine metagenomics, targeted genetic editing, and ecological modeling to understand and reshape soil microbiomes. This work has implications for sustainable agriculture, climate change mitigation, and food security.

Livestock Methane Reduction

Livestock, particularly cattle, are significant contributors to global methane emissions through enteric fermentation. We target rumen microbiomes to decrease methane production while maintaining or improving animal health and productivity.

Using CRISPR-based tools and phage technologies, we selectively modify or eliminate methanogenic archaea in the rumen. Our goal is to develop practical, scalable interventions that can significantly reduce the carbon footprint of livestock agriculture.

Gut Microbiome & Health

The human gut microbiome influences numerous aspects of health, from metabolism and immunity to neurological function. We develop tools for precise editing of gut microbial communities to treat microbiome-associated diseases.

Our research encompasses understanding disease-associated microbial signatures, developing delivery systems for microbiome-editing tools, and creating next-generation therapeutics that can restore healthy microbiome function.

Machine Learning for Editing Tools

We leverage artificial intelligence and machine learning to accelerate the development of microbiome editing technologies. Our computational approaches enable rapid identification of targets, prediction of editing outcomes, and optimization of intervention strategies.

By integrating large-scale microbiome datasets with advanced ML models, we can predict community dynamics, design more effective CRISPR guides, and identify novel therapeutic targets across diverse microbial ecosystems.
